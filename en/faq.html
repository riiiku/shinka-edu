Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about studying in Japan, answered

Application Process
The typical process is: Sign up → Profile evaluation → School & professor selection → Document preparation (research proposal, PS, etc.) → Professor outreach (for kenkyusei) → Formal application → Interview coaching → Admission → Visa processing. SGU programs follow an online application system similar to Western universities, while the kenkyusei route requires securing professor approval first. The entire cycle takes 3-12 months.
SGU (Super Global University) is a government initiative encompassing English-taught programs at 37 of Japan's top universities. No Japanese skills are needed — apply and study entirely in English. Compared to Japanese-taught programs, SGU admissions resemble Western-style applications (online application + essays + interview) and are quite competitive.
"Nainei" is Japan's unique informal acceptance system — a professor agrees to take you as their research student before the formal review. After receiving nainei, you still need to complete the formal application (submit documents, pay the examination fee), but the pass rate is very high. Think of it as "the professor has said yes" but the university paperwork still needs to happen.
We recommend starting 6-12 months before your target enrollment. SGU programs have fixed deadlines (typically November to March for fall enrollment), while kenkyusei outreach is more flexible. If you need to prepare for language tests (TOEFL/IELTS/N1), plan even earlier. Starting early always beats starting late.
Costs & Fees
National university tuition is about 535,800 JPY/year (~$4,000 USD). Private universities range from 800,000-1,500,000 JPY/year. Living costs vary by city: Tokyo runs about $8,000-12,000 USD/year, while other cities cost $5,000-8,000. Japan allows international students to work part-time (up to 28 hours/week), which can help cover living expenses.
SGU Programs
Popular schools include UTokyo (ITASIA, MPP, GSP, GPSS), Waseda (GSICCS, EDICS, GSAPS), Keio (KMD, SFC), Nagoya (GSID), and Tohoku (GPEM). Programs cover social sciences, public policy, international relations, media design, economics, environmental studies, and more. STEM SGU options are fewer but growing.
Requirements vary by program, but typical minimums include: bachelor's degree (16 years of education), English scores (TOEFL 90+ or IELTS 6.5+ as baseline; top programs prefer 100+/7.0+), and GPA 3.0+ as a safe threshold. Some programs require GRE/GMAT. Beyond numbers, the quality of your research proposal/PS and research fit with the program are equally important.
Documents & Materials
The research proposal is the most critical document in Japan graduate admissions, typically 800-1200 characters (Japanese) or 1000-1500 words (English). It must include: research background and problem statement, literature review, research methodology, and expected outcomes. The most important element is showing clear alignment between your research and the target professor/department's focus. Our essay advisors will help you develop your research framework from scratch.
Most programs require 2-3 recommendation letters. Best options: professors in your research field, your thesis supervisor, or instructors who know your academic abilities well. If you have work experience, consider adding one from a supervisor. The key is that recommenders should genuinely know you and write substantive, specific endorsements — not just generic praise.
Visa & Logistics
Japan's student visa process (Certificate of Eligibility + visa application) is quite standardized, and approval rates are high when documents are complete. After admission, your school applies for a Certificate of Eligibility (COE), which you then use at the Japanese embassy to obtain your visa — typically issued within 1-2 weeks. We assist with the entire visa process after admission.
Japanese graduate schools typically have two enrollment periods: April (spring, most common) and October (fall, common for SGU programs). After receiving your offer, you need to complete visa processing and arrive before the enrollment date. There's usually a 2-6 month buffer between the admission notice and enrollment.
